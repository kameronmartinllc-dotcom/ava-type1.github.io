üìã Understanding Your Coverage

Key Terms to Know:

  • Deductible: Amount you pay before insurance kicks in
  • Copay: Fixed amount you pay for each service
  • Coinsurance: Percentage you pay after deductible
  • Out-of-pocket maximum: Most you'll pay in a year
  • Prior authorization: Insurance approval needed for certain supplies

üíä Covered Supplies (Usually)

  • Blood glucose meters and test strips
  • Insulin and syringes/pen needles
  • Lancets and lancing devices
  • Ketone test strips
  • Glucagon emergency kits
  • CGM systems (with prior authorization)
  • Insulin pumps (with prior authorization)

üìû Questions to Ask Your Insurance

  • What's my deductible and out-of-pocket maximum?
  • Do I need prior authorization for CGM/pump?
  • Which pharmacies are in-network?
  • What's the copay for diabetes supplies?
  • Are there quantity limits on test strips?
  • Do I need a referral to see an endocrinologist?
  • What's covered for diabetes education?

üìù Prior Authorization Process

  1. Get prescription from your doctor
  2. Submit to insurance with medical justification
  3. Wait for approval (usually 5-10 business days)
  4. If denied, appeal with additional documentation
  5. Work with your doctor to provide more details

‚úÖ Appeals Process

If your claim is denied:

  • Request written explanation of denial
  • Gather medical records and doctor's letters
  • Submit formal appeal within deadline
  • Consider external review if internal appeal fails
  • Get help from patient advocacy groups

üí∞ Cost-Saving Tips

  • Use generic insulin when possible
  • Buy supplies in bulk (90-day supply)
  • Compare prices at different pharmacies
  • Use manufacturer coupons and programs
  • Consider mail-order pharmacy
  • Ask about patient assistance programs
